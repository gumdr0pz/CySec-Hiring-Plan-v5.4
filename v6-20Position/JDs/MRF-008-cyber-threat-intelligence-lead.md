## 1. Role Purpose

The Cyber Threat Intelligence Specialist anchors the BU's CTI capability while owning the GovSec platform and its product offerings end to end. This dual-function role transforms raw indicators and global threat signals into actionable intelligence that protects clients, and simultaneously governs the GovSec product roadmap — translating public-sector security and compliance needs into deliverable, well-governed solutions. By bridging threat intelligence tradecraft with product ownership, the role ensures the GovSec platform is intelligence-driven, compliance-aligned, and sequenced for sustainable delivery across Malaysian government and critical-infrastructure clients.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

**Threat Intelligence Operations**

- **Establish and manage** the BU's end-to-end threat intelligence operations, defining collection priorities, sources, and tradecraft that align to client and business needs.
- **Develop and govern** structured threat intelligence frameworks (e.g. the intelligence cycle, MITRE ATT&CK, Diamond Model, F3EAD) to ensure consistent, defensible analytical outputs.
- **Lead** proactive threat hunting and analysis activities, correlating internal telemetry with external intelligence to surface emerging adversary tactics, techniques, and campaigns.
- **Produce** clear, decision-ready intelligence reports, briefings, and advisories tailored to technical teams, executives, and external clients.
- **Operate and optimise** the threat intelligence platform (MISP, OpenCTI), ensuring high-quality enrichment, deduplication, and timely dissemination of indicators across the organisation.
- **Track and assess** geopolitical and sector-specific threats relevant to Malaysian and regional clients, translating context into prioritised risk guidance.

**GovSec Platform & Product Ownership**

- **Own the product roadmap** for the GovSec platform and its government-security offerings, prioritising features and engagements against client value, compliance obligations, and BU strategy.
- **Operate and improve** the GovSec platform day to day, driving platform enhancements, stability, and feature delivery aligned to client outcomes.
- **Define and manage requirements**, translating government client needs and regulatory mandates into clear product specifications, user stories, and acceptance criteria.
- **Govern compliance outcomes**, ensuring the GovSec platform aligns with Malaysian government security frameworks and applicable regulatory mandates.
- **Coordinate with regulatory bodies and government authorities** as a trusted point of contact for audits, certifications, and compliance reviews.
- **Manage the backlog and release planning**, sequencing delivery with the cyber-core and general delivery teams to agreed outcomes and SOW commitments.

**Cross-Functional Collaboration**

- **Collaborate** with offensive security, DevSecOps, blockchain security, and consulting teams to embed intelligence-driven insight and platform capabilities into engagements, detections, and client deliverables.
- **Engage government and enterprise stakeholders**, translating technical security findings and platform capabilities into clear, decision-ready briefings for senior and non-technical audiences.
- **Maintain platform and product documentation, reporting, and quality controls** to ensure full traceability and audit readiness across the intelligence and product lifecycles.
- **Mentor and develop** analysts within the CTI team, raising analytical rigour, writing standards, and operational maturity across the function.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Cybersecurity, Intelligence, Computer Science, or a related field.
- 7+ years of professional experience in threat intelligence, government security, or security product/programme ownership.
- Demonstrated hands-on experience with threat intelligence platforms such as MISP and OpenCTI.
- Strong analytical reasoning and excellent written communication skills, with a track record of producing intelligence reports for varied audiences.
- Active CISSP, CISM, or equivalent industry certification.
- Working knowledge of Malaysian government security frameworks and regulatory expectations.
- Holds CTI, GCTI, or an equivalent threat intelligence certification (see Section 7).

## 4. Preferred Qualifications

- Master's degree in a relevant field (Cybersecurity, Intelligence Studies, International Relations, or similar).
- Product management certification (e.g., Pragmatic, SAFe POPM) or equivalent product-ownership experience.
- Prior experience working within or directly with the government sector or law enforcement / intelligence agencies.
- Working knowledge of geopolitical threats and their cyber implications.
- Experience supporting critical infrastructure protection (CIP) initiatives.
- Proficiency in one or more foreign languages to support open-source collection and analysis.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- Threat intelligence platforms: MISP, OpenCTI (administration, tuning, and integration).
- Intelligence frameworks and models: MITRE ATT&CK, Diamond Model, Cyber Kill Chain, F3EAD, and the intelligence cycle.
- Threat hunting and analysis across endpoint, network, and log telemetry.
- Indicator lifecycle management: collection, enrichment, normalisation, and dissemination (STIX/TAXII).
- Open-source intelligence (OSINT) tradecraft and dark/deep web research.
- Information security governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) practices.
- Security frameworks and standards: ISO/IEC 27001, NIST CSF, Malaysian public-sector security guidelines.
- Product-management skills: roadmapping, requirements/backlog management, and prioritisation.
- Security risk assessment methodologies and remediation planning.
- Familiarity with SIEM, EDR, and detection engineering workflows.
- Product/backlog tooling (e.g., Jira, Confluence) and agile delivery practices.
